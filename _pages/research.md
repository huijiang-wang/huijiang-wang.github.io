## Intelligent Soft Robotics

This research investigates how mechanical design, compliant materials, and embodied dynamics can produce adaptive behavior without relying exclusively on complex control. Huijiang Wang's doctoral thesis examined intrinsic embodied dynamics for adaptive robotic behaviors, while later work explored bio-inspired variable-stiffness mechanisms and adaptive robot perching. The common objective is to treat morphology and passive physical properties as active parts of a robotic system's intelligence, linking model-based analysis with experimentally grounded design.

**Keywords:** embodied dynamics, soft robotics, variable stiffness, adaptive behavior, bio-inspired design

**Representative work:** variable-stiffness anthropomorphic fingers; bio-inspired variable-stiffness design for adaptive robot perching; reduced-order modeling for a variable-stiffness piano-playing finger.

## Soft Grippers and Resilient Manipulation

Huijiang Wang develops soft and form-flexible grippers for reliable interaction with objects of varied geometry and material properties. His work includes self-closing suction grippers for industrial grasping, a self-regulated self-healing gripper, and industrial studies of soft universal gripping. These systems combine compliant structures, material behavior, and task-oriented design to improve adaptability and resilience. Related multi-material suction-cup research further examines how self-closing and self-healing mechanisms can reduce energy use in vacuum gripping.

**Keywords:** soft grippers, suction grasping, self-healing materials, industrial manipulation, form-flexible design

**Representative publications:** "Self-Closing Suction Grippers for Industrial Grasping via Form-Flexible Design" and "Self-Regulated Self-Healing Robotic Gripper for Resilient and Adaptive Grasping."

## Dexterous Robotic Hands and Expressive Manipulation

This theme studies the design and control of anthropomorphic hands for tasks that require coordinated, expressive motion. Piano playing provides a demanding test case for investigating passive properties, variable stiffness, hand morphology, and data-driven simulation. The work spans anthropomorphic hand design, reduced-order finger models, expressive piano-playing simulation, and neuro-musculoskeletal modeling of human upper-limb coordination. Together, these studies examine how task requirements and anthropomorphism can be balanced in dexterous robotic systems.

**Keywords:** dexterous manipulation, anthropomorphic hands, piano-playing robots, passive dynamics, neuro-musculoskeletal modeling

**Representative publications:** "Balancing Anthropomorphism and Task Specificity for Dexterous Hand Design" and "Data-driven simulation framework for expressive piano playing by anthropomorphic hand with variable passive properties."

## Robotic Sensing and Embodied Intelligence

Huijiang Wang's sensing research connects physical deformation, material response, and environmental interaction with usable robot state information. It includes a review of touchless sensing for soft robots, fibre-optic sensing for integrated spatial-status measurement and colour expression, and sensing-integrated soft robotic systems. This work treats sensing as part of the robot's body rather than as an isolated subsystem, supporting designs in which structure, perception, and actuation are developed together.

**Keywords:** soft-robot sensing, touchless sensing, fibre-optic sensors, integrated perception, material intelligence

**Representative publications:** "Touchless Sensing for Soft Robots: A Review" and "Integrated sensing and colour expression of spatial status using a fibre optic sensor pair."

## Human-Robot Collaboration and Learning

This research explores collaborative behavior in tasks where robots must respond to people in real time. Huijiang Wang developed a learning-based accompaniment system for cooperative piano playing and has investigated emotional alignment in human-robot musical tasks. Related work on low-latency robot-assisted hand-object interaction combines procedural organization with responsive control. These projects connect human-inspired task structure, learning, and embodied design to support coordinated interaction between people and robotic systems.

**Keywords:** human-robot collaboration, real-time accompaniment, robot learning, hand-object interaction, musical robotics

**Representative publication:** "Human-Robot Cooperative Piano Playing with Learning-Based Real-Time Music Accompaniment."
